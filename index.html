🧠 What Is This Strategy, In Plain English?

Imagine a market says there's a 30% chance of an event — but your data says 55%. The crowd is wrong. You buy the contract cheap, and when the market corrects itself, your position is worth more. This scanner finds those gaps automatically across economics, politics, and crypto markets — where mispricings happen most.

1

Get a Kalshi Account

Go to kalshi.com and sign up free. It's the regulated US exchange powering Robinhood's event contracts. Start with as little as $50.

2

Get Your API Key

In Kalshi, go to Settings → API Keys and create a key. Paste it into the Scanner tab to enable live market data.

kalshi.com → Account → Settings → API Keys → Create
3

Set Your Threshold i
Going above 20%: Real edges this large are extremely rare on liquid markets. You'll rarely see flags, and when you do they're likely stale or illiquid contracts you can't actually trade at the quoted price.

Going below 6%: Most of these "edges" are just normal bid-ask spread noise and market microstructure — not genuine mispricings. You'll overtrade and fees will erode any theoretical profit.

Default is 8% — the minimum gap needed to flag a trade. Too low = noise. Too high = missed opportunities. Start between 8–12%.

4

Scanner Flags It — You Decide

Each card shows market price vs fair value, your edge %, and a Kelly Criterion bet size. You place the trade on Kalshi manually.

5

Wait, Then Exit

Markets drift toward true probability over time. Sell early or hold to resolution. Buy at 30¢, resolve YES = 70¢ profit per contract.

6

Track & Calibrate

After 20–30 trades, check if your underpriced calls resolved YES more often. If yes, your model works. If not, refine your fair value estimates.

Key Terms, Simply Explained
Market Price (¢)
What the crowd pays. 30¢ means they think there's a 30% chance of YES.
Fair Value (¢)
What your data suggests the real probability is. 55¢ = 55% chance.
Edge %
The gap between the two. 30¢ vs 55¢ = 25% edge. Bigger is better.
Kelly Criterion
Math formula for the ideal bet size. Prevents overbetting even on strong edges.
Contracts
What you buy on Kalshi. Each YES pays $1 if the event happens, $0 if not.
Resolution
When the market closes and Kalshi settles all contracts automatically.
Liquidity
How easy to enter/exit. Stick to markets with $10k+ volume.
Mispricing
When the crowd gets probabilities wrong. Happens most in low-attention markets.
